Complete Guide to the Cultivation of Peach Farming in India


Feb 20, 2023


Peach is an important seed fruit crop of temperate regions mainly in hilly areas such as J & K, Meghalaya, Uttarakhand, Sikkim, etc. Fruits are rich in protein, sugar, minerals, and vitamins. It has various uses as a fresh fruit as well as a processed product. Do you know about the process of Peach Farming? If not, Mera Farmhouse can guide you about it by talking to an experienced peach farmer Mr. Harman Randhawa. He can guide us through everything about peach cultivation.


Type of soil & climate required for cultivation

Peach crops require deep sandy loam soil with high organic matter content and good drainage. For this, the pH of the soil should be 5.8 and 6.8 Acidic and salty soil is not suitable for peach crops. For its cultivation, gently sloping land is considered suitable Its fruits take better texture in foothills, high-hills, and general areas. Peach fruit crop requires a clear, clean, and warmer atmosphere at the time of sowing. Also, during the fruiting stage, a minimum temp. around 25 ºC is required for well ripening of peach fruits and a rain of 200-300mm is also required.


Sleeping period of Peaches

Peach plants are deciduous in nature and “go to sleep” in winter from Nov to Jan. As the winter season comes to an end pink the color flower starts growing then the fruit set takes place from 20 to 25 Feb. The harvesting of the fruit is done in April & May.


How is its cultivation done?

The cultivation of the fruit is done by planting plants. It is a fruit of the stone family like pear and plum and is a long-term fruit so you should buy its plant from a certified nursery. You should consult the horticulture department to buy the best quality plants. Mr. Randhawa told us that you shouldn’t compromise on the plant as you need the right quality.

How & why Started This Peach farming?

Mr. Randhawa was doing kinnow farming earlier but the season ends in Nov to Feb and income stops. So he decided to start farming which can help him earn after the kinnow season. They started peach farming, they are growing peaches on one farm, pear and guava on the second farm which starts giving income after the peach season, and again when this season ends then again Guava season starts. You can earn a good amount of income but you need patience. He has grown 7.5 acres of peach farm and another farm of 14 acres of peach.


Difference between Peach and Apricot

Peach

  • It is round shaped stone family fruit
  • It comes in pink, yellow, and red color variations
  • It has velvet-textured skin with fuzz
  • They are bigger in size and juicy
  • Peach is a fruit with a lot of nutrients and many health benefits

    • Apricot

  • It is oval shaped stone family fruit
  • They vary from yellow to orange
  • It is smooth but lacks fuzz
  • It is small in size and less juicy than peaches
  • It helps to improve digestion and keeps body hydrated


    • Health benefits of Peach fruit

    * helps in eye health & skin health * helps in weight loss due to its high fiber content * Prevents cancer * control high cholesterol * improve heart health

    Varieties of Peach in India

    * TA-170, Flordasun, Shan-e- Punjab and Sharbati


    How to take care of peach orchards?

    Peach fruit is less affected by pests or diseases. You need to remove the weeds manually and this fruit requires less maintenance is required. After planting the trees, irrigation is done. Drip irrigation is the right way and can be done during dry spells. This is a 2–3-month crop which you can manage and take care of easily.  The peaches turn red on ripening and you can start harvesting the fruit.


    How much investment is required to start this farming?

    Mr. Randhawa told us that you can easily start this farming with a small investment. You can start it on 4 Kanal farms and firstly you need to learn about this fruit farming in detail. Thereafter you can grow it on a larger scale.


    The fruit is in great demand and has a good scope. Anyone can start this business as very less farming of peaches is in today’s time. Even if the production scale increases, you can store the fruit and sell it for pudding purposes. In simple words, this fruit can help you earn in the long run.
